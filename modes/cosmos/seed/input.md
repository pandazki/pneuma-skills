# The Antiqued Map

The shop closed at eight, but Eliot had stayed past midnight. The chiming of the long-case clock — three sharp strokes — was the only witness to his quiet panic. He had been cataloging the late Mrs. Halloran's estate for three days, and tonight, beneath the bottom drawer of her writing desk, he had found a map.

Not just any map. A 1742 chart of the New England coast, hand-tinted and signed by Joseph Blanchard — a name Eliot recognized only from the auction house's "Most Wanted" list. He turned it over slowly, his breath catching when he saw what was sketched on the verso: a small, deliberate "X" off the coast of Marblehead, with a single annotation in a different ink: *"Three fathoms beneath, ask Reuben."*

Eliot's first thought was forgery. Mrs. Halloran had collected oddities, and her late husband, Tobias, had been a known prankster among the maritime societies. But Reuben? Reuben Halloran was Tobias's uncle — and according to the family records Eliot had already processed, Reuben had been the captain of a brigantine called the *Halcyon* that had vanished off Marblehead in 1849.

He sat down. Outside, the rain had become a steady drum on the awning.

The forgery theory had three flaws. First, the ink Blanchard had used on the front of the map was iron gall — the verso annotation was walnut-and-soot, which a forger of Tobias's era wouldn't have known to mix. Second, the watermark of the paper was authentic for 1740-1745 imports through the port of Salem; Eliot had matched it against three known Blanchard charts that very afternoon. Third — and this was the point that made his pulse race — the desk itself, with its concealed compartment, was Tobias's. Tobias had not been a prankster about Tobias's own desk.

Someone had hidden this. Recently.

Eliot photographed both sides of the map. He logged the find in the estate manifest under a placeholder code (he was supposed to do that for everything anyway), then locked it back in the drawer. As he was leaving, he caught his own reflection in the shop window: a tired man holding too many questions. *Three fathoms beneath, ask Reuben.* A man who had been dead a hundred and seventy-six years.
